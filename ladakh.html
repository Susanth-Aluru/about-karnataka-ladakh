Introduction

Hemis Tsechu is a 2-day grand Buddist festival celebrated by the Hemis Gompa Monastery in Leh, Ladakh.
It is celebrated on the tenth day of the Tibetan calendar,
generally during the months of June or July. It marks the birthday of the revered spiritual savior, Guru Padmasambhava, also called Guru Rinpoche. The festival signifies the triumph of good over evil, just like Dussehra.

Origin

Guru Padmasambhava, a powerful spiritual leader, brought tantric Buddhism to the Himalayas in the 8th century.
He freed the people from demons and evil spirits, and had a major impact on the spread of
Buddhism in Tibet.Guru Padmasambhava appeared in eight different forms, each with their own teaching style.The Guru Padmasambhava festival is a two-day celebration held at Hemis Gompa, the largest monastery in Ladakh. The festival features dances, music, and performances. People from all over the world come to see the festival. The monastery is decorated with Buddhist paintings and statues of Buddha and Guru Padmasambhava. The festival is a time of devotion and respect for Guru Padmasambhava.

Festivities

In this 2 day festival,people celebrated it with dance, music and performances.
The Gompa is visited by a wide range of people, which include foreingers,
indians and the locals.The monastary is filled with thangkas Buddist paintings and large statues of Buddha and Guru Padmasambhava.During this festival,two monks in front of the flagpole blow Dungchen, a long trumpet and dance in a masked dance form called Lamas.
